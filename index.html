Hackathon + Games = GameJam

The purpose of RGU GameJam is to present an opportunity for students to develop a computer or board game within 30 hours based on a given theme. The GameJam is not just a competition, but an event oriented towards having fun, networking, and experiencing mentorship from academic staff and industry members.

Workshops

Skill-building activities and events to develop your abilities in game design over the course of the two day GameJam!

Mentorship

Guidance from academics and industry representatives on a range of topics to help make your game!

Prizes

Compete against opposing teams on themed challenges to win wonderful prizes and become the GameMaster!

Guidance for Participants

  • Location and Date: The Sir Ian Wood Building at Robert Gordon University on Saturday March 30th until Sunday 31st of March.
  • Cost: The cost of taking part in the GameJam is Â£5 per participant. This will cover registration and supper on both days. Please note that participants should bring their own breakfast and lunch.
  • Event Length: The opening ceremony will start at 9am on Saturday 30th March. The GameJam will last 30 hours, starting at approx 9.30am on Saturday and ending at 15.30pm on Sunday. The event will end following the awards ceremony at 17:00.
  • Team Size: Formation of teams is encouraged! Note the maximum team size is 5 participants.
  • Registration: Registration is available at the above link. Note that teams must register in advance. The closing date of registrations is Thursday 28th March at 16:00.
  • Eligibility: This event caters to aspiring game developers, board game enthusiasts and experienced hackathon goers from any university or college. That said, this event is strictly limited to students. An identifier will be worn at all times.
  • Staffing: Staff members from the School of Computing at RGU will be present at all times throughout the event.
  • Equipment: Please bring your own equipment (including a laptop and any software you plan to use). A small amount of specialist equipment will be available to borrow, but will be shared between all participants. Note that ID will be required to borrow any equipment.
  • Sleeping Arrangements: As a two day event, teams will be taking breaks over the course of the GameJam. This will involve going home to sleep. We will provide quiet rooms for rest, but these are strictly BYOP-B (Bring-Your-Own_Pillow-and-Blanket)!
